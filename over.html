Verbs with Over

Click on a verb to show/hide definitions and examples.

to review (or) to examine, inspect briefly
1. I looked over my notes before the exam.
2. Look over the report and tell me what you think.
3. Dave needs someone to look over his work.

to recover from (sickness or bad situation); to overcome (a difficulty)
1. Paula will get over the flu in a few days
2. He will never get over his fear of flying.
3. It will take Spain years to get over the economic crisis.

to take control
1. I will take over for my boss when he retires.
2. He's tired so you will need to take over driving for a while.
3. His intention was to take over the company.

to escalate out of control (tensions, emotions)
1. Racial tensions have boiled over in Nebraska.
2. Anger boiled over after Donald Trump's election.
3. Tensions in Syria boiled over into violent protest.

to pass responsibility/power to someone else.

1. Obama handed over presidency to Donald Trump in 2017.
2. He will soon hand over to a new director.
3. The dictator will not hand over power to his opponent.

to finish or come to an end

1. The meeting will be over in 20 minutes.
2. What time will the class be over?
3. The war is over!

Listen to the audio and take note all the phrasal verbs you here.

Frank: Hi Linda, I’m so sorry I’m late. I ran into an old friend on the street. We started chatting and I completely forgot the time, is the meeting over?

Linda: Hey Frank, I thought you were never going to show up. Yes, the meeting is over. But go into the meeting room and pick up the notes I wrote down for you. They’re on the table.

Frank: Ok, thanks, I’ll take a look over the notes before lunch. How was the meeting?

Linda: I don’t have time to go into details now but there was a lot of tension between the boss and their representatives and the situation quickly boiled over into a big argument. The negotiations broke down at that point and they said they would prefer to close down the business rather than let our company take over.

Frank: Wow! We’ve offered a lot of money to take over the company. Will they really turn it down because they got into argument with our boss?

Linda: You know how difficult it can be to deal with our boss, I think they have finally run out of patience with him. The situation has turned into a nightmare. They will need to work out their differences soon or this deal will never happen.

Frank: If we lose this deal, the president will never get over it. He’s wanted to buy this company for five years. Did you find out why he hasn’t been to the office this week?

Linda: The president? Yes, he has decided to take some time off due to stress. His doctor told him to cut down on work and take up a hobby to help him relax. They say that he is going to hand over power to his son for the rest of the year while he recovers.

Frank: Really? I had no idea about that. Ok well, I’m going to go down stairs and buy a coffee at the café. Apparently, they’re giving out free bagels! You want anything?

Linda: No, I’m fine, I just had a sandwich. We’ll talk more later. Bye!

Frank: Ok bye Linda, I’ll see you later.
