Verbs with Down

Click on a verb to show/hide definitions and examples.

to reject an offer
1. $50,000 is a hard offer to turn down.
2. He turned down a promotion because he didn't want the stress.
3. She's so pretty no man has ever turned down a date with her.

to stop functioning
1. His car broke down in the middle of the highway.
2. After 10 years, my computer has finally broken down.
3. Talks between the US and China have broken down

to reduce volume of something
1. He has cut down on smoking.
2. We need to cut down on advertising expenses.
3. You should cut down on sugar.

to close permanently
1. The restaurant had to close down due to poor hygiene standards
2. His accountant has advised him to close down his business.
3. The factory has closed down after opening five years ago.

to take note

1. You'll never remember if you don't write it down.
2. Get a pen and write down this phone number.
3. He wrote down everything the teacher said.

to descend

1. House prices have come down (gone down) a lot this year.
2. The sun goes down at around 9pm in summer.
3. You need to go down the stairs to reach the basement.
